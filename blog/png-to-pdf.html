How to Convert PNG to PDF Online for Free

📅 April 4, 2026 ⏱ 4 min read ✍️ BuildPDF Team

PNG (Portable Network Graphics) is the go-to format for screenshots, logos, illustrations, and any image that benefits from lossless compression and transparent backgrounds. But when you need to share, print, or submit a PNG professionally, PDF is almost always the right format to send.

This guide shows you how to convert PNG files to PDF instantly, for free, using BuildPDF — no software to install and no files sent to a server.

Step-by-Step: Convert PNG to PDF

01

Visit BuildPDF

Navigate to buildpdf.co in Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari, or Brave.

02

Upload your PNG file(s)

Drag and drop your .png file onto the upload zone, or click "Choose Files." You can select multiple PNGs to merge them into one PDF.

03

Configure your output

Select page size (A4 or Letter are most common), orientation, and margin. For graphics-heavy PNGs, use High (95%) image quality.

04

Convert and download

Click "Convert to PDF." The file is generated in your browser and the download starts immediately — no waiting, no queues.

⚠️ Heads up about transparency: PDF doesn't natively support transparent backgrounds. If your PNG has a transparent background, it will be converted to white in the PDF output. This is standard behavior across all PDF converters.

When Should You Use PNG vs JPG?

Understanding the difference between PNG and JPG helps you choose the right source file:

  • Use PNG when: your image has sharp edges, text, logos, screenshots, transparent areas, or diagrams — PNG's lossless compression keeps these crisp.
  • Use JPG when: your image is a photograph — JPG handles photographic gradients very well at small file sizes.

When converting either format to PDF with BuildPDF, the result quality primarily depends on the resolution of your original file, not the format itself.

Common Use Cases for PNG to PDF

  • Submitting a screenshot — many institutions require documents as PDFs, not image files.
  • Packaging design assets — convert a logo or graphic mockup to PDF for printing or client delivery.
  • Creating a certificate or printable — PNGs designed in Canva or Photoshop can be converted to print-ready PDFs.
  • Merging multiple screenshots — collect a series of UI screenshots into a single walkthrough PDF.
  • Document scanning — scan documents on your phone as PNGs and consolidate them into a PDF.

WEBP and Other Formats

BuildPDF also supports WEBP, GIF, and BMP images — not just JPG and PNG. The conversion process is exactly the same; just drag and drop your file and follow the same steps above.

💡 Tip: If you're converting a high-resolution PNG (e.g., a 4K screenshot), the resulting PDF may be large. Use Medium (80%) quality if you need a smaller file size for email attachments.

Privacy & Security

Your PNG files are processed entirely within your browser's memory. No file is ever sent to our servers. Once you close or refresh the page, nothing is retained. This makes BuildPDF especially appropriate for screenshots containing sensitive information, like banking details, personal IDs, or private messages.
